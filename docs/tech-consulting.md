# Tech Consulting Playbook

**A 12-week guide to launching a tech consulting business in Singapore — from zero to paying clients.**

Your business model has four revenue streams, in priority order:

| # | Stream | Revenue | Scalability |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Website hosting & management | $128–298/mo per client | High (templates) |
| 2 | Plio SaaS upsell | +$100–298/mo per client | Very high (multi-tenant) |
| 3 | Light tool integrations | +$30–50/mo per client | High (off-the-shelf tools) |
| 4 | Custom builds (selective) | $3k–8k + $200/mo | Low (your time) |

---

## The Business Model

### The "Digital Rental Model"

You are a **Digital Landlord.** You don't sell websites — you sell a managed digital presence on a monthly subscription. You own the domain, the repo, the hosting. If a client stops paying, their site goes down.

**Do not say:** "I will build you a free website."
**Say:** "I will waive the $2,800 design fee if you subscribe to my Digital Management Package."

### Why This Works in Singapore

1. **The Digital Gap:** Profitable businesses (tuition centres, TCM clinics, salons, neighbourhood shops) still rely on a Facebook page from 2018 or a Carousell listing.
2. **Low Effort for You:** A static site takes 3–4 hours using your reusable template.
3. **High Perceived Value:** To a business owner, a professional website looks like $2,000+ of work.

## Revenue Stream 2: Plio SaaS Upsell

Plio is your scheduling and management SaaS. It's a modular platform that works for any service business — not locked to a single vertical.

**How it works:**
- Businesses pick the modules they need during onboarding: Dashboard, Calendar, Clients, Services, Team, Rooms, Invoicing, Booking, Settings
- 5 pre-built templates handle industry-specific defaults: **Tuition Centre**, **Music School**, **Yoga Studio**, **Wellness Centre**, **General**
- The calendar supports both recurring classes (e.g., weekly tuition) and bookable appointments (e.g., salon slots)

### The "Trojan Horse" Strategy

**Month 1:** Launch the client's website. Focus on delivering a great static site.

**Month 2:** Send them a traffic report. "Hey, 50 people clicked the 'Call' button this month!"

**Month 3 (The Pivot):**
> "I noticed you're getting a lot of inquiries. Are you spending time replying to WhatsApp messages about prices and slots?
>
> I have a scheduling and management system called Plio that we can add to this website. It lets customers book automatically and you manage everything from one dashboard.
>
> Since I already manage your site, I can add it for just +$100/month."

Now you've moved them from a $198/mo website client to a $298/mo SaaS client.

### Plio Pricing (Upsell Add-On)

| | Add-on to Starter/Growth | Included in Pro |
|---|---|---|
| Plio | +$100/mo | Included |

### When to Upsell by Client Type

Plio works for any service business. During onboarding, the client picks a template (Tuition Centre, Music School, Yoga Studio, Wellness Centre, or General) and enables the modules they need. The template pre-configures labels and defaults — no custom development required.

| Client type | Website tier | Upsell |
|---|---|---|
| Tuition centre / enrichment | Growth or Pro | Plio — Tuition Centre template |
| Music school | Growth or Pro | Plio — Music School template |
| Clinic / salon / spa | Growth or Pro | Plio — Wellness Centre template |
| Yoga / Pilates studio | Growth or Pro | Plio — Yoga Studio template |
| Any other service business | Growth or Pro | Plio — General template |

## Revenue Stream 4: Custom Builds (Selective)

Custom development work — only offered when a client specifically asks for something your templates and Plio can't cover.

### Guardrails

| Rule | Why |
|---|---|
| **Never pitch it** — only offer when asked | Protect your time |
| **Minimum $3,000** | Filters small requests that should be a tier upgrade |
| **Fixed scope, fixed price** | No hourly billing, no scope creep |
| **Max 1 custom project at a time** | Protect your Plio development time |
| **Mandatory $200/mo maintenance** | Keep the recurring revenue |
| **Deposit: 50% upfront** | De-risk your time investment |

### When to Say Yes

A clinic client says: "I need an internal dashboard where staff can see today's appointments, patient history, and stock levels."

That's beyond Plio's scope. Quote them $5,000 + $200/mo, build it in 2–3 weeks, move on.

### When to Say No

- "Can you build me something like Grab?" → No.
- "I need an e-commerce store." → Point them to Shopify.
- "Can you build a mobile app?" → Not yet.
- Any project that would take more than 3 weeks of your time → No.

## 12-Week Execution Plan

### Phase 1: Foundation (Weeks 1–3)

**Goal:** Business is registered, tools are set up, template is built, portfolio site is live.

#### Week 1: Business Setup

- [ ] **Register your business with ACRA**
  - Sole proprietorship is sufficient to start ($115 registration fee, $15/year renewal)
  - Choose a business name (e.g., "[YourName] Digital" or a brand name)
  - Register at [BizFile+](https://www.bizfile.gov.sg)
  - Processing takes 15 minutes to 2 days
- [ ] **Open a business bank account**
  - DBS Business Account or OCBC Business Account (both have free options for sole proprietors)
  - You need this for PayNow Business (receiving payments by UEN)
- [ ] **Set up invoicing**
  - Use **Wave** (free) or **Xero** ($30/mo — better for GST when you hit $1M revenue threshold)
  - Create your first invoice template with your business details
- [ ] **Get a business phone number**
  - A second SIM or a WhatsApp Business account on your existing number
  - Set up auto-reply: "Thanks for reaching out! I'll get back to you within 2 hours."
- [ ] **Set up a simple contract template**
  - Use the contract checklist below
  - Have it reviewed by a friend in legal if possible, otherwise use it as-is

#### Week 2: Template Build Sprint

- [ ] **Choose your stack**
  - **Framework:** Astro (recommended for static) or Next.js (static export)
  - **Styling:** Tailwind CSS
  - **Hosting:** Vercel (free tier is sufficient for static sites)
  - **CMS:** None. Hardcode the text. Client changes go through you — this justifies the monthly fee.
- [ ] **Build your reusable template**
  - Hero section with business name, tagline, hero image
  - About section
  - Services / courses list
  - Contact section with WhatsApp floating button (`https://wa.me/65XXXXXXXX`)
  - Google Maps embed
  - Footer with address, hours, links
  - Mobile-responsive (test on your phone)
- [ ] **Create 2–3 industry variants**
  - Tuition centre variant (class schedule, subjects offered)
  - Clinic/salon variant (services menu, booking CTA)
  - General service business variant (portfolio, testimonials)
- [ ] **Set up your deployment pipeline**
  - One Vercel project per client (free tier supports multiple projects)
  - Domain: register via Namecheap or Cloudflare (~$15/year per .com)
  - You register the domain under your account — you are the Digital Landlord

#### Week 3: Portfolio & Presence

- [ ] **Build your own portfolio site**
  - Use the same template system
  - Show 2–3 "demo" sites (the industry variants you built in Week 2)
  - Include: your services, pricing (show the 3 tiers), a contact form, testimonials (even if empty for now)
- [ ] **Set up Google Business Profile for yourself**
  - Category: "Web design" or "Marketing consultant"
  - This is how local businesses will find you later
- [ ] **Prepare your pitch materials**
  - A 1-page PDF summarizing your offer (send via WhatsApp after initial conversation)
  - Before/after screenshots of your demo sites
  - A short explainer you can show on your phone

### Phase 2: Prospecting & First Clients (Weeks 4–8)

**Goal:** Build a prospect list, start outreach, close 2–3 clients.

#### Week 4: Build Your Prospect List

- [ ] **Walk the neighbourhood**
  - Visit 3–5 areas with high small-business density (HDB shop lots, neighbourhood malls)
  - Look for: tuition centres, clinics, salons, bakeries, studios — with no website or a bad one
  - Note down: business name, phone number, what they sell, whether they have a website
  - Check their Google Maps listing — if it's unclaimed or sparse, that's your opportunity
- [ ] **Online research**
  - Search Google Maps for "[business type] near [area]" — filter for businesses with no website link
  - Browse Instagram / Facebook for local businesses with active social media but no website
  - Check Carousell for service businesses advertising there (they need a website upgrade)
- [ ] **Personal network audit**
  - List friends, family, former classmates who own or know someone who owns a business
  - Don't pitch yet — just identify who might be warm leads
- [ ] **Target: 20–30 prospects on your list by end of week**

#### Week 5–6: Start Outreach

Use different scripts for different channels:

**Walk-in Script (Cold):**
> "Hi, I'm [Name]. I'm a local web developer building my portfolio in [Area].
>
> I tried searching for your business on Google to send to my friend, but I couldn't find a website.
>
> I'd like to build a professional website for you. I normally charge $2,500 for design, but I'll waive that completely — you'd just cover the hosting at $128 a month to keep it online. It includes Google Maps setup so people can find you.
>
> Can I show you a demo on my phone?"

**Online DM Script (Instagram/Facebook):**
> "Hi [Name], I've been following your page — love your [products/classes/services]!
>
> I noticed you don't have a website yet. I'm a local developer and I help small businesses in SG get found on Google.
>
> I have a special offer: I waive the design fee ($2,500 value) and you just cover hosting ($128/mo). Includes Google Maps optimization.
>
> Would you be open to a quick chat? I can show you a demo I made."

**Personal Network Script (Warm):**
> "Hey [Name], I've started a digital consulting business.
>
> I'm helping local businesses get a professional website and Google presence. Do you know any business owners who might be interested? I'm offering the first few clients a waived setup fee."

**Outreach cadence:**
- 3–5 walk-ins per week
- 5–10 online DMs per week
- 2–3 personal network asks per week
- **Track everything** in a simple spreadsheet: Name, Business, Channel, Date, Response, Status

#### Week 7–8: Close & Onboard

When a prospect says yes:

1. **Send the 1-page PDF** summarizing the offer and pricing tiers
2. **Recommend the Growth tier** ($198/mo quarterly) — explain the value of the traffic report
3. **Collect the setup fee** ($488) and first quarter payment ($534) via PayNow or bank transfer
4. **Sign the contract** (digital signature via email is fine)
5. **Begin the onboarding process** (see Phase 3)

**Goal by Week 8:** 2–3 signed clients.

**Revenue projection with 3 Growth clients:**
- Setup fees: 3 × $488 = **$1,464** (one-time)
- Monthly recurring: 3 × $198 = **$594/mo**
- Quarterly billing: 3 × $534 = **$1,602/quarter**

### Phase 3: Deliver & Upsell (Weeks 9–12)

**Goal:** Deliver websites, set up reporting, begin Plio upsell pipeline.

#### Week 9–10: Client Delivery

For each client, follow this workflow:

1. **Content collection (Day 1–2)**
   - Send a WhatsApp message: "I need the following to build your site:"
     - Business name and tagline
     - 3–5 photos (storefront, products/services, team)
     - List of services/courses with brief descriptions
     - Address, phone number, operating hours
     - Any existing logo (or you'll use their business name as text)
   - Set a deadline: "Please send by [3 days from now] so I can launch by [date]."

2. **Build (Day 3–5)**
   - Clone your template, swap in their content
   - Register their domain (under your account)
   - Set up Google Business Profile (or optimize their existing one)
   - Add WhatsApp floating button
   - Deploy to Vercel
   - Total time: 3–4 hours per client

3. **Review & Launch (Day 6–7)**
   - Send the client a preview link
   - Allow 1 round of revisions (text changes, photo swaps only)
   - After approval, point the domain and go live
   - Send a "Your website is live!" message with the URL

4. **Post-launch (Day 8)**
   - Set up Google Analytics or Vercel Analytics
   - Submit the site to Google Search Console
   - Send the client a screenshot: "Your site is now on Google!"

#### Week 11: Monthly Reporting System

Set up a simple reporting workflow:

- **End of each month:** Pull basic stats (page views, button clicks, Google Business views)
- **Send a 3-line WhatsApp message:**
  > "Hi [Name], here's your monthly website report:
  > - 230 people visited your site
  > - 45 clicked the WhatsApp button
  > - Your Google listing was viewed 180 times
  >
  > Let me know if you'd like to discuss ways to get more inquiries!"

This report does two things:
1. Justifies your monthly fee ("I'm actively managing this")
2. Sets up the Plio upsell ("you're getting inquiries — let me help you automate")

#### Week 12: Begin Plio Upsell Conversations

For any service business getting inquiries through their website:

**Tuition centre / enrichment client:**
> "I noticed you're getting a lot of parent inquiries through the website. Are you still managing class schedules and enrollment manually?
>
> I have a scheduling and management system that handles class timetables, student enrollment, attendance, and even parent communication — all in one dashboard. I can add it to your site for +$100/month."

**Clinic / salon client:**
> "Your booking inquiries have been growing! Are you still managing appointments over WhatsApp?
>
> I have a scheduling and management system that lets clients see your availability and book online. It handles your service menu, practitioner schedules, and sends automatic reminders. I can add it for +$100/month."

**Any other service business:**
> "You're getting a lot of inquiries — are you still managing bookings manually?
>
> I have a management system I can plug into your site. Customers book online, you see everything in one dashboard — calendar, clients, invoicing. I can add it for +$100/month."

For clients who only need lightweight tools, offer managed integrations instead (Cal.com, Google Reviews, etc.).

## Scope Creep Protection

Since you're offering a low setup fee, clients will try to get unlimited work. Protect yourself:

### The Template Policy

1. **"I work from a library of high-performance templates."** Do not design from scratch.
2. **"Includes 1 round of text/photo revisions."** After that, changes count against their monthly update hours.
3. **"Static content only."** No e-commerce, no login, no database — unless they upgrade to Plio or a custom build.

### What's Included vs Extra

| Included in monthly fee | Extra (billed hourly or as upgrade) |
|---|---|
| Text changes (within monthly hours) | New pages beyond tier limit |
| Photo swaps | Custom animations or interactions |
| Google Business updates | E-commerce functionality |
| Basic SEO maintenance | Logo design |
| SSL and hosting | Photography |
| Monthly report (Growth/Pro) | Social media management |

### Hourly Rate for Extra Work

$80/hour for work beyond the included monthly hours. Bill in 30-minute increments. Always quote before starting.

## Contract Essentials

Your contract should include these clauses. Keep it to 2 pages maximum — nobody reads long contracts.

### Must-Have Clauses

1. **Parties:** Your business name and their business name
2. **Services:** Website design, hosting, and management (reference the specific tier)
3. **Fees:** Monthly/quarterly/annual amount, setup fee, payment terms
4. **Payment method:** PayNow or bank transfer, due on the 1st of each billing period
5. **Term:** Minimum 12 months, auto-renews unless cancelled with 30 days notice
6. **Ownership:**
   - You own the domain registration, source code, and hosting account
   - Client owns their content (text, photos, logos)
   - Upon termination and full payment, you will transfer the domain to the client
7. **Updates:** Included hours per month (as per tier), additional work at $80/hour
8. **Late payment:** 7-day grace → reminder → site offline at 14 days
9. **Early termination:** Remaining months charged at 50%
10. **Liability:** You are not liable for business losses due to website downtime
11. **PDPA:** You will handle their customer data (if any) in compliance with Singapore PDPA

### Optional But Recommended

- **Non-compete:** Client agrees not to hire you away to a competitor during the contract term (unlikely but protects you)
- **Referral bonus:** Client gets 1 free month for every referral that signs up

## Tech Stack for Client Websites

### Keep It Static

Do **not** use WordPress. It gets hacked, plugins break, and it's slow. You are a React developer — use your strengths.

| Layer | Tool |
|---|---|
| Framework | **Astro** (recommended) or **Next.js** (static export) |
| Styling | Tailwind CSS |
| Hosting | Vercel (free tier per project) |
| Domain | Namecheap or Cloudflare (~$15/year per .com) |
| Analytics | Vercel Analytics (free) or Google Analytics |
| CMS | None — hardcode the text. Changes go through you. |

### Why No CMS

If clients can edit their own site, they don't need you. The moment you give them a WordPress login, your $198/month fee becomes unjustifiable. By keeping the content in code, you are the gatekeeper — and that's exactly what you want.

## Key Principles

1. **You are a Digital Landlord, not a freelancer.** You own the infrastructure. Clients rent access.
2. **Recurring revenue over project fees.** A $198/mo client paying for 2 years is worth $4,752 — more than most one-time projects.
3. **Templates over custom work.** Every hour spent on custom design is an hour not spent on Plio or prospecting.
4. **Template-first.** Plio works for any service business — templates handle the customization. No custom development per vertical.
5. **Upsell through trust.** The website is the foot in the door. Plio is the real business. Don't rush the upsell — earn the relationship first.
6. **Protect your time.** Scope creep kills consulting businesses. Be friendly but firm about boundaries.
